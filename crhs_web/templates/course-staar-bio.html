STAAR Testing Information

All public schools in Texas require students from grades 3 -12 to take STAAR tests. The STAAR tests are the state's testing based on state curriculum standards in core subjects including reading, writing, mathematics, science, and social studies.

The tests are given in the spring as the school year concludes at the school the student attends in a regular classroom. If the student requires special accommodations they may be moved to a different area.

The STAAR test is designed to measure what the students are learning in each grade and whether or not they are ready for the next grade. If you do not pass the STAAR test then you cannot graduate high school. This class is focused on preparing you pass the Biology STAAR exam as well as preparing you for AP Biology.
